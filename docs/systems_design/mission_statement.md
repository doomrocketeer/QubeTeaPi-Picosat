# Mission Statement
QubeTeaPi is an open-source, PocketQube-format satellite bus and design standard that serves to provide a low-cost platform for makers, educators, students, and businesses to launch a payload into orbit.
It shall use the RP2040 microcontroller as the on-board computer (OBC), and feature a bill-of-materials (BOM) cost of under $100. It shall be designed to operate in Low Earth Orbits (LEO) and Sun-synchronous Orbits (SSO). The lifespan of a typical mission should be 1 year.
It shall feature further essential modules, including an electrical power system (EPS) and communications system (COMMS) on a single main PCB (called the "pastry"). The pastry shall feature a bus populated with edgeboard connectors to interface with additional modules (called "fillings"). Different pastries should be available in 1-3P sizes. Typical fillings could include:
- A high-performance OBC for AI applications
- An attitude determination and control system (ADCS)
- A high-power COMMS module
- Optical and imaging payloads
- Scientific and experimental payloads
